RE-ANNOUNCEMENT OF FY 2000 COMPETITIVE GRANT FUNDS
FOR SERVICE AREA NY-5 (Chautauqua County, New York)

The Legal Services Corporation (LSC) is re-announcing the availability of calendar year 2000 grant funds for service area NY-5 (Chautauqua County, New York) to provide high quality civil legal services to eligible clients. The grant term is anticipated to begin as early as July 1, 2000 and end December 31, 2000 (six months). The grant amount is $76,760 for NY-5 for the six-month grant period.

As a result of LSC's multi-year funding policy, a two year funding cycle is available for service areas in New York. The second grant term is for calendar year 2001 (twelve months). Successful applicants for 2000 funding will be eligible to submit a grant renewal application for calendar year 2001 funding for service area NY-5 in New York.

The Corporation is soliciting grant proposals from interested parties who are qualified to provide effective, efficient, and high quality civil legal services to eligible clients in the specified service area. Nonprofit organizations that have as a purpose the furnishing of legal assistance to eligible clients, private attorneys, groups of private attorneys or law firms, state or local governments, and substate regional planning and coordination agencies are eligible to apply.

The Request for Proposal (RFP) will be available starting February 18, 2000. Pursuant to the LSC regulation governing competition (45 CFR 1634), a Notice of Intent to Compete must be filed. The deadline for receipt of the Notice is April 3, 2000, 5 p.m. EDT. Prospective applicants are encouraged to read the regulation carefully. Responses to the RFP are due April 28, 2000, 5 p.m. EDT.
