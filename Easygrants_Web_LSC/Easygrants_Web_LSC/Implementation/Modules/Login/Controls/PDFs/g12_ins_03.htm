LSC year 2003 Grants Competition

Instructions for Projected Expenditures by Type of Activity -- Form G-12

This section contains instructions for the following client services form:
Form G-12 Projected Expenditures by Type of Activity

Accessing the Forms: The LSC Grant Application Forms are accessible for all applicants who have submitted the Notice of Intent to Compete (Form H). To access the forms, click Grants Applications from the main Legal Services Corporation Applicant Information Network (www.ain.lsc.gov) screen. This will take you to the Competitive Grants Applications menu. Then select Grant Application Forms and enter your LSC Applicant ID and the Password you used for submitting the Notice of Intent to Compete form.

After you have entered your Applicant ID and password, you will access the Grant Application Forms menu. Select the Client Services - Form G and click 'PROCEED' to continue. Then you need to select a service area. The service area screen will contain a Drop-Down box which contains a listing of service areas you entered in your Notice of Intent to Compete Form. To select a service area, click on the service area identifier, then click on the 'OK' button at the bottom of the screen.

After you have selected a service area, you will access the Client Services Forms menu which contains the following items:
(1) Instructions and Definitions
(2) Projected 2003 Expenditures - Form G-12

To select one of these items, click in the circular button to the left of the item, then click on the 'REPORT' button at the bottom of the screen.

For LSC to obtain accurate information from these forms, all applicants must use consistent terms and concepts when compiling and submitting data. Refer to the Client Services Definitions at the end of these instructions for necessary guidance. You can view and print the Instructions and Definitions for your easy reference.

Please note that applicants which apply for more than one service area must complete a separate set of forms for each service area.

Scrolling Forms. Because the Client Services forms are larger than a single screen will contain, each form has a scroll bar along the right-hand margin of the screen to enable you to see the rest of the form. Clicking on the scroll bar will enable you to scroll down through a form. 

Entering Data Each form has certain information pre-filled, including your organization's name and LSC Applicant ID. Each form will contain cells for entering client services information, such as numbers of cases. To enter this information, click on the cell in which you want to enter information, then type in the information. You may also use the Tab key to move to the next cell in a form. Pressing the Shift and Tab keys simultaneously will enable you to move to the previous cell.

Numeric Data. Some forms require entry of numeric information. These forms will not work properly with non-numeric data such as commas or spaces. Please do not enter commas or periods in numeric data, or leave a cell blank instead of entering zero. Entering commas, periods or any non-numeric data will cause an error message while saving. The system will not allow you to save such data. 

Saving Data. Each form has buttons at the bottom for Saving, Submitting, and Exiting. Please be sure to press the SAVE button prior to exiting the form on which you have been working. The EXIT button does not save information which you have entered. Pressing the SAVE button also calculates or recalculates any totals in the form. You may press the SAVE button at any time to save information which you have entered and recalculate totals.

Exiting Forms. After saving the information which you have entered, you may click on the EXIT button to leave the form on which you have been working. You may exit from and return to any form as many times as you wish, provided that you have not clicked on the SUBMIT button.

Printing Forms: You can use your browser to print the form that you are working on. Click on the print icon at the top of the screen when you are ready to print and click on 'OK' to print.

Submitting Forms. Once you are certain that a form on which you have been working is complete, you may submit it to LSC by clicking on the SUBMIT button. Please do not press the SUBMIT button unless you are certain that you no longer wish to work on the form. After you have submitted a form to LSC, you will be unable to access it without calling LSC to ask that we un-submit the form.

Form G-12 Projected Expenditures by Type of Activity

This form must be completed by all applicants. It collects data regarding projected calendar year 2003 expenditures for cases, matters, and supporting activities, and for cases in each legal area. The total expenditures on this form should correspond to the "Total LSC expenditures" reported on Form D-12 -- line 27, column 1.

Applicants should prepare a separate Form G-12 for each service area.

In Column I Report projected year 2003 expenditures in dollars.
In Column II This is a calculated field and reflects the percentages of projected year 2003 expenditures in Column I.
Note: The sum of the percentages in Section A, Rows 1, 2, and 3 should equal 100%--total LSC expenditures. The sum of the percentage in Section B, Rows 1 through 10 should equal 100% -- total LSC expenditures for cases.
In Section A Report projected year 2003 LSC expenditures for Cases, Matters, and Supporting Activities. The entry on Row A. 4 in Column I should be identical to the entry on line 27, Column 1 on Form D-12.
In Section B Report projected year 2003 LSC expenditures for Case Types. As noted, the entry in Column I Row 11, "Total Case Funding," should be the same as the entry in Column I, Row A.1.

Client Services -- Definitions

Client Service includes all forms of advice, brief service and extended service to low-income clients in cases involving all types of legal problems.

Limited Service includes counsel, advice, brief service, referral after legal assessment, and cases where there was insufficient merit to proceed, client withdrawal, client failure to return and change in eligibility status.

Extended Service includes negotiation toward settlement (with or without litigation), administrative agency proceedings, litigation in court and other services beyond limited service, including transactional services which do not involve a controversy, and thus are not "settled" or litigated. Examples of such transactional services include drafting complex wills and contracts (simple ones would be classified as Brief Service), obtaining licenses or permits, purchasing or selling real estate and obtaining small business loans.  Thus, cases in CSR Categories F, G, H, I and K should be considered "Extended Services."  For further information about CSR categories, please refer to the LSC CSR Handbook.

Client Services Provided through PAI include services provided as part of the applicant's planned activities to meet the PAI requirements of 45 CFR Part 1614 of the LSC Regulations.

Type of Legal Problem: The list of types of legal problems, by definition, encompasses all types of cases that will be handled with Corporation funds.

Consumer Finance includes, but is not limited to, bankruptcy, debt relief, collection, contracts, warranties, credit access, energy, loans, public utilities, and unfair sales practices.

Education includes, but is not limited to, special education, suspension, and expulsion.

Employment includes, but is not limited to, job discrimination and wage claims.

Family includes, but is not limited to, adoption, custody, visitation, divorce, separation, guardianship, name change, parental rights termination, paternity, spouse abuse, and support.

Juvenile includes, but is not limited to delinquency, neglect and abuse.

Health includes, but is not limited to, Medicaid and Medicare.

Housing includes, but is not limited to, federally subsidized housing, other public housing, home ownership, real property and landlord/tenant.

Income Maintenance includes, but is not limited to, AFDC, welfare, black lung, food stamps, Social Security, SSI, unemployment compensation, veterans benefits and workers compensation.

Individual Rights includes, but is not limited to, immigration, naturalization, mental health, prisoner's rights and the rights of the disabled.

Miscellaneous includes, but is not limited to, incorporation, dissolution, Native American tribal law, licenses (auto and others), torts, wills and estates.

Cases are services in which assistance is provided to one or more specific eligible clients. Examples include representation in litigation, administrative proceedings and negotiations, and such actions as advice, providing brief services and transactional assistance, and assistance with individual PAI cases.

Matters are actions that contribute to the overall delivery of services that do not involve direct legal advice to, or legal representation of, one or more specific clients. Examples include: special project meetings about legal services to the community; community education events; pro se clinics; providing information about the availability of legal assistance; developing informational materials; training; continuing legal education; general supervision of applicant services; the preparation and dissemination of desk manuals; PAI recruitment; and other direct or indirect applicant services that are not actions taken in cases.

Supporting Activities are actions that are administrative in nature and are not cases or matters. Such actions include fundraising and actions classified as "management and general" for accounting purposes. Examples of supporting activities include: fundraising; board meetings; staff breaks; general staff meeting; researching timekeeping systems; and staff evaluations.
