Instructions for Staffing Forms -- Form E

This section contains instructions for the following staffing forms:
Form E-12(a) Projected Staffing - Recipient Only
Form E-12(c) Projected Staffing - Subrecipient Only

Accessing the Forms: The LSC Grant Application Forms are accessible for all applicants who have submitted the Notice of Intent to Compete (Form H). To access the forms, click Grants Applications from the main Legal Services Corporation Applicant Information Network (www.ain.lsc.gov) screen. This will take you to the Competitive Grants Applications menu. Then select Grant Application Forms and enter your LSC Applicant Id and the Password you used for submitting the Notice of Intent to Compete form.

After you have entered your Applicant ID and password, you will access the Grant Application Forms menu. You can then select the Staffing Forms and click 'OK' to continue.

The next screen displays the Office and Staffing Forms menu as follows:
Instructions and Definitions
Projected Staffing - Recipient Only
Projected Staffing - Subrecipient Only

To select, click in the circular button to the left of the item and click 'OK' to continue.

Scrolling Forms: Because these forms are larger than a single screen will contain, each form has a scroll bar along the right-hand margin of the screen to enable you to see the rest of the form. Clicking on the scroll bar will enable you to scroll down through a form. You may also use the Page-Down and Down-Arrow keys to scroll down through a form. Similarly, you may use the scroll bar or Page-Up and Up-Arrow keys to scroll back up through any form.

Entering Data: Each form has certain information pre-filled, like your organization's name and LSC Applicant ID. Each form will contain cells for entering information. To enter data, click on the cell in which you want and type in the information. You can use the Tab key to move to the next cell in a form. Pressing the Shift and Tab keys simultaneously will enable you to move to the previous cell.

Numeric Data: Some forms require entry of numeric information. These forms will not work properly with non-numeric data such as commas or spaces. Please do not enter commas or periods in numeric data, or leave a cell blank instead of entering zero. Entering commas, periods or any non-numeric data will cause an error message while saving. The system will not allow you to save such data.

Saving Data: Each form has buttons at the bottom for Saving and Exiting. Please be sure to press the SAVE button prior to exiting the form on which you have been working. The EXIT button does not save information which you have entered. You may press the SAVE button at any time to save information which you have entered.

Exiting Forms: After saving the information which you have entered, you may click on the EXIT button to leave the form on which you have been working. You may exit from and return to any form as many times as you wish, provided that you have not clicked on the SUBMIT button.

Printing Forms: You can use your browser to print the form that you are working on. Click on the print icon at the top of the screen when you are ready to print and click on OK to print.

Submitting Forms:   Once you are certain that a form on which you have been working is complete, you may submit it to LSC by clicking on the SUBMIT button. Please do not press the SUBMIT button unless you are certain that you no longer wish to work on the form. After you have submitted a form to LSC, you will be unable to access it without calling LSC to ask that we un-submit the form.

Projected Staffing Forms - Form E-12

All applicants should complete this form.

The projected staffing information for the recipient main and branch offices is to be combined and reported in a single form, which is Form E-12(a). For subrecipient office projection use Form E-12(c).

Report projected staff under the LSC grant. Only report staffing information for individuals who will be delivering civil legal assistance to low-income clients under the grant.

Staff that are to be partially funded under the LSC grant and partially funded by other sources should be counted as "part-time" for purposes of Form E-12. Do not report pro bono attorneys projected to be participating in the applicant's Private Attorney Involvement program on this form.

Form E-12(a) - Projected Recipient Staffing

This form captures information on projected recipient staffing for calendar year 2001. Please include in this form information for all recipient main and branch offices combined, but do not include information on subrecipient offices. Information on subrecipient offices is reported separately in Form E-12(c)

Form E-12(c) - Projected Subrecipient Staffing

This form captures information on projected subrecipient staffing levels for calendar year 2001. Please include in this form information for all subrecipient offices combined, but do not include information on recipient main and branch offices. Information on recipient main and branch offices is reported separately in Form E-12(a)

Job Classification Codes -- Definitions

01 Director: This category refers only to the chief executive officer.

03 Deputy Director: This category refers to the deputy executive officer.

05 Director of Litigation: This category refers to the executive responsible for oversight of litigation.

07 Managing Attorney: This category refers to attorneys who supervise legal work and have substantial administrative and financial responsibilities, e.g., administer a cost center, branch office, or the like.

09 Supervising Attorney: This category refers to attorneys how supervise legal work but do not have substantial administrative and financial responsibilities.

11 Staff Attorney: This category includes all other attorneys, as well as law school graduates who have not passed a bar examination.

13 Paralegal: This category includes professionals whose duties consist primarily of such activities as intake interviewing, case investigations, checking court records, legal research, client representation at administrative hearings, and outreach and community work.

15 PAI Coordinator: This category refers to a person who devotes a major portion of time to coordination of the Recipient's PAI plan.

17 Training Responsible Person: This category refers to an individual who devotes a major portion of time coordinating or providing training for the Recipient's or subrecipient's staff.

18 Financial Professionals: This category refers to persons who assist the director with financial management. For example, controller, accountant or bookkeeper.

19 Management Professionals: This category refers to persons who assist the director with personnel or administrative management. It does not include managing attorney positions covered in job code 07 or financial professionals covered in job code 18.

21 Law Clerk: This category refers to law students who have not graduated from law school.

23 Senior Aide: This category refers to persons employed under a specially designed job program for older workers.

25 Administrative Assistant: This category refers to executive assistants and administrative aides who do not have substantial administrative and financial responsibilities, but whose duties exceed those delegated to legal secretaries and clerical workers.

27 Secretarial/Clerical: This category includes all legal secretaries, as well as clerical workers such as typists, file clerks and receptionists.
