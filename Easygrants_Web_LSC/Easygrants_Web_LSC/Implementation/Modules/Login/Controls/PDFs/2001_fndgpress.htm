LEGAL SERVICES CORPORATION ANNOUNCES FY 2001
FUNDING DECISIONS

WASHINGTON -- Legal Services Corporation (LSC) today announced the results of its FY 2001 grant competition, with service areas in eighteen states, Guam, Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, and the District of Columbia eligible for competitively awarded grants.

As a result of the competitive process:

  • three-year grants will be awarded for service areas in six states: Colorado, Illinois, Indiana, Pennsylvania, Wyoming, and most programs in California.
  • two-year grants will be awarded for service areas in eight states: California, Iowa, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota, Oregon, South Dakota, two service areas in Virginia, and Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
  • one-year grants will be awarded to service areas in six states: Kentucky, Michigan, North Carolina, the remaining service areas in Virginia, West Virginia, and Wisconsin, and in the District of Columbia.
  • all migrant service areas were in competition for FY 2001; in most instances migrant grant awards are of the same duration as basic field awards.

Competition for service areas occurred in two states. In California, San Fernando Valley Neighborhood Legal Services Inc. competed with Legal Services Program for Pasadena and San Gabriel-Pomona. San Fernando Valley Neighborhood Legal Services Inc. will receive the three-year grant. Virginia Legal Aid Society, Inc. competed with the Legal Aid Society of New River Valley Inc. The two-year grant will be awarded to Southwest Virginia Legal Aid Society, Inc. 

In 1995, and again in 1998, LSC required all legal services programs to begin or to continue planning processes to develop more effective statewide legal services systems. A compulsory element of the planning process is an examination of the number of LSC programs in a particular state to determine the most effective configuration for delivering legal services. Variations in grant terms are made to further encourage programs in these states to continue developing methods to enhance effective statewide delivery systems. 

For FY 2001, the Congress has approved a $330 million appropriation for LSC, a $25 million increase over last year’s budget.
