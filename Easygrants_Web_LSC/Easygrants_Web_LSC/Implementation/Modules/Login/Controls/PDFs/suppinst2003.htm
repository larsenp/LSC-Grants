LSC Year 2003 Grants Competition

Instructions for Supplemental Forms
(New Applicants Only)


This section contains instructions for the following  forms:
Form D-2 -- Current Year Expenses
Form D-4 -- Current Year Support and Revenue
Form E-1 -- Current Year Staffing
Form G-1 -- Current Year Client Services

Accessing the Forms: The LSC Grant Application Forms are accessible for all applicants who have submitted the Notice of Intent to Compete (Form H).  To access the forms, click Grants Applications from the main Legal Services Corporation Applicant Information Network (www.ain.lsc.gov) screen. This will take you to the Competitive Grants Applications menu. Then select Grant Application Forms and enter your LSC Applicant Id and the password you used for submitting the Notice of Intent to Compete form.

After you have entered your Applicant ID and password, you will access the Grant Application Forms menu. You can then select the Supplemental Forms and click 'OK' to continue.

The next screen displays the Supplemental Forms menu as follows:
Instructions and Definitions
Expenses - Form D-2
Revenue - Form D-4
Staffing - Form E-1
Client Services  - Form G-1

To select, click in the circular button to the left of the item and click 'OK' to continue.

For LSC to obtain accurate information, all applicants must use the same definitions in supplying data for each form.   Please refer to the Definitions at the end of these instructions when entering data to the various forms.  

Scrolling Forms: Because these forms are larger than a single screen will contain, each form has a scroll bar along the right-hand margin of the screen to enable you to see the rest of the form. Clicking on the scroll bar will enable you to scroll down through a form. You may also use the Page-Down and Down-Arrow keys to scroll down through a form. Similarly, you may use the scroll bar or Page-Up and Up-Arrow keys to scroll back up through any form.

Entering Data: Each form has certain information pre-filled, like your organization's name and LSC Applicant ID. Each form will contain cells for entering information. To enter data, click on the cell in which you want and type in the information. You can use the Tab key to move to the next cell in a form. Pressing the Shift and Tab keys simultaneously will enable you to move to the previous cell.

Numeric Data: Some forms require entry of numeric information. These forms will not work properly with non-numeric data such as commas or spaces. Please do not enter commas or periods in numeric data, or leave a cell blank instead of entering zero. Entering commas, periods or any non-numeric data will cause an error message while saving. The system will not allow you to save such data.

Saving Data: Each form has buttons at the bottom for Saving and Exiting. Please be sure to press the SAVE button prior to exiting the form on which you have been working. The EXIT button does not save information which you have entered. You may press the SAVE button at any time to save information which you have entered.

Exiting Forms: After saving the information which you have entered, you may click on the EXIT button to leave the form on which you have been working. You may exit from and return to any form as many times as you wish, provided that you have not clicked on the SUBMIT button.

Printing Forms: You can use your browser to print the form that you are working on. Click on the print icon at the top of the screen when you are ready to print and click on OK to print.

Submitting Forms:  Once you are certain that a form on which you have been working is complete, you may submit it to LSC by clicking on the SUBMIT button. As always, remember to print the forms before submitting. Please do not press the SUBMIT button unless you are certain that you no longer wish to work on the form. After you have submitted a form to LSC, you will be unable to access it without calling LSC to ask that we un-submit the form.

I. Form D-2 - Current Year Projected Expenses

This form captures projected non-LSC expenses for calendar year 2002 . All new applicants must complete Form D-2.

Report total current year projected expenses for calendar year 2002 from the following sources of non-LSC funding:

Title XX of the Social Security Act , Older Americans Act, Violence Against Women, Other Federal Grants, Community Development Block Grants. State Grants, Local Grants, United Way, Foundation Grants, Bar Association Grants, IOLTA, Attorney Fee Awards, Carryover Funds, Publication Income, Other Non-LSC funds

Law firms and governmental entity applicants should include expenses incurred against public or private grants or contracts to serve low-income clients.

Complete this data based on applicant's best estimate of expenses that will be incurred in providing services to low-income clients.

Refer to the Expense Definitions at the bottom of these instructions.

 

II. Form D-4 - Current Year Projected Support and Revenue

This form captures current year projected total support and revenue for calendar year 2002 . All new applicants must complete Form D-4.

SECTION (A):

1. State and local government applicants & substate regional planning or coordinating agency applicants should report total projected revenue for civil legal assistance activities from all sources using the categories designated in this section.
2. Attorney and law firm applicants that provided civil legal assistance and were funded to do so by public, private, or other grants, should report total support and revenue from state and local grants, private foundations, IOLTA, attorney fee awards, carryover funds, publications' income, and other sources.
3. All applicants should include any remaining amounts on Line 99, "Other Funds," that are not otherwise captured under the specific categories of this section. Other appropriated funds should also be reported on Line 99.

SECTION (B):

1. Attorney and law firm applicants that provided civil legal assistance but were not funded to do so by public or private grants, should gross fees and receipts from client services. Also, report total gross receipts from other income earned in for-profit activities.
2. All other applicants should not complete this section

SECTION (C):

This section reflects total current year projected 2002 support and revenue for the service area and it is a calculated field which adds the amounts in sections (A) and (B).

For descriptions of the terms refer to Support and Revenue Definitions at the end of the instructions.

 

III. Form E-1 - Current Year Staffing

All new applicants should complete this form.  This form captures information on attorneys and other staff for the current year by age, gender and ethnicity. Form E-1 also captures information about average years of experience and average salaries.

 

IV. Form G-1 - Current Year Client Services

This form captures information on calendar year 2002 client services.

In Column (1) Report the number of cases that will be closed during the year by "limited service."
In Column (2) Report the number of cases that will be closed during the year with the provision of "extended service."
In Column (3) Report the number of cases that will remain open at year end
In Column (4) This is the total number of cases for the year which is the sum of columns (1), (2) & (3)

For descriptions of the terms refer to Client Services Definitions at the end of the instructions.

Expense Definitions

1 Lawyers: This category should include all salaries, wages and profits paid to attorneys (other than for contract services), whether employed directly or supervised by the recipient and whether part-time, full-time, or temporary. It also includes law school graduates who have not passed a bar examination.

2 Paralegals: This category should include all salaries and wages paid to paralegals, whether employed directly or supervised by the Recipient (e.g., senior aides) and whether part-time, full-time, or temporary.

3 Other Staff: This category should include all salaries and wages paid to all other staff, whether administrative/clerical staff, students, or others, and whether full-time, part-time, or temporary (e.g., Work/Study).

4 Employee Benefits: This category should include all fringe benefits paid on behalf of employees, including retirement, FICA, health and life insurance, worker's compensation, disability insurance, unemployment insurance, day care, and other payroll-related costs.

6 Space: This category includes rent or lease expenses and mortgage payments which are reported separately. It also includes, under "Other Expenses," janitorial, maintenance, and utility expenses.

9 Equipment Rental: This category includes lease or rental expenses for office furniture, fixtures, and equipment (except telephone). It also includes an estimate of maintenance costs for that equipment, whether pursuant to a service contract or an estimate of individual repair bills.

10 Office Supplies: This category includes all basic office accessories and supplies, including paper materials and supplies used for copiers. Printing and postage are also to be included in this category. All equipment purchases which cost less than $100 may be placed in this line item.

21 Property Acquisition: This category includes equipment, library purchases and other major expenses that occur infrequently, and which are capitalized (e.g., major renovations).

25 Other: This category includes all remaining applicant expenses that are not otherwise identified in these definitions.

 

Support and Revenue Definitions

30 Title XX: Federal/state funds available under Title XX of the Social Security Act. The funding agency is usually the state welfare, human resources, or social services agency.

32 Older Americans Act: Funds available under the Older Americans Act (usually Title III). Providers include state or area aging agencies and/or the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services/Office of Development Services/Administration on Aging.

33  Violence Against Women Act: Funds available under the Violence Against Women Act, including V-STOP and STOP grants. Providers include the U.S. Department of Justice and state and local agencies such as state attorneys general.

34 Other Federal Grants: Other sources of federal funding, including funds received for job training, community action, or community education.

36 Community Development Block Grants: Block grant funds under the Community Development Act administered by HUD. The funds are usually obtained from city or county agencies administering community development programs.

40 State Grants: Public funds received from state agencies, exclusive of those included in the federal categories listed above, or IOLTA category listed below.

42 Local Grants: Public funds from city or county agencies, except those included in the categories listed above.

44 United Way: Funds from United Way, Community Chest, Red Feather, United Givers Fund, or other consolidated community fund-raising organizations.

46 Foundation Grants: Funds from any private foundation, local, regional, or national.

48 Bar Association Grants: Contributions from bar associations and related organizations, but not IOLTA.

50 IOLTA: Interest On Lawyer's Trust Accounts.

52 Attorney Fee Awards/Non-LSC Case(s): The amount received or expected to be received as the result of a judgment, court order, or settlement of cases.

54 Non-LSC Carryover Funds: Funds, other than LSC funds, that are unexpended from a previous accounting period or calendar year.

55 Non-LSC Publication Income: Income earned from the sale of publications produced with non-LSC funds.

57 Gross Fees and Receipts from Client Services: Income earned from fees paid and other receipts attributable to for-profit operation as a law firm.

58 Other Income Earned in For-Profit Activities: Income earned in for-profit operations other than income for legal services to clients.

99 Other Funds: This category includes all remaining support, revenue, or appropriated funding sources that are not identified in these definitions.

Client Services Definitions

Client Service includes all forms of advice, brief service and extended service to low-income clients in cases involving all types of legal problems.

Limited Service includes counsel, advice, brief service, referral after legal assessment, and cases where there was insufficient merit to proceed, client withdrawal, client failure to return and change in eligibility status.

Extended Service includes negotiation toward settlement (with or without litigation), administrative agency proceedings, litigation in court and other services beyond limited service, including transactional services which do not involve a controversy, and thus are not "settled" or litigated. Examples of such transactional services include drafting complex wills and contracts (simple ones would be classified as Brief Service), obtaining licenses or permits, purchasing or selling real estate and obtaining small business loans.  Thus, cases in CSR Categories F, G, H, I and K should be considered "Extended Services."  For further information about CSR categories, please refer to the LSC CSR Handbook.

Client Services Provided through PAI include services provided as part of the applicant's planned activities to meet the PAI requirements of 45 CFR Part 1614 of the LSC Regulations.

Type of Legal Problem: The list of types of legal problems, by definition, encompasses all types of cases that will be handled with Corporation funds.

Consumer Finance includes, but is not limited to, bankruptcy, debt relief, collection, contracts, warranties, credit access, energy, loans, public utilities, and unfair sales practices.

Education includes, but is not limited to, special education, suspension, and expulsion.

Employment includes, but is not limited to, job discrimination and wage claims.

Family includes, but is not limited to, adoption, custody, visitation, divorce, separation, guardianship, name change, parental rights termination, paternity, spouse abuse, and support.

Juvenile includes, but is not limited to delinquency, neglect and abuse.

Health includes, but is not limited to, Medicaid and Medicare.

Housing includes, but is not limited to, federally subsidized housing, other public housing, home ownership, real property and landlord/tenant.

Income Maintenance includes, but is not limited to, AFDC, welfare, black lung, food stamps, Social Security, SSI, unemployment compensation, veterans benefits and workers compensation.

Individual Rights includes, but is not limited to, immigration, naturalization, mental health, prisoner's rights and the rights of the disabled.

Miscellaneous includes, but is not limited to, incorporation, dissolution, Native American tribal law, licenses (auto and others), torts, wills and estates.

Cases are services in which assistance is provided to one or more specific eligible clients. Examples include representation in litigation, administrative proceedings and negotiations, and such actions as advice, providing brief services and transactional assistance, and assistance with individual PAI cases.

Matters are actions that contribute to the overall delivery of services that do not involve direct legal advice to, or legal representation of, one or more specific clients. Examples include: special project meetings about legal services to the community; community education events; pro se clinics; providing information about the availability of legal assistance; developing informational materials; training; continuing legal education; general supervision of applicant services; the preparation and dissemination of desk manuals; PAI recruitment; and other direct or indirect applicant services that are not actions taken in cases.

Supporting Activities are actions that are administrative in nature and are not cases or matters. Such actions include fundraising and actions classified as "management and general" for accounting purposes. Examples of supporting activities include: fundraising; board meetings; staff breaks; general staff meeting; researching timekeeping systems; and staff evaluations.
