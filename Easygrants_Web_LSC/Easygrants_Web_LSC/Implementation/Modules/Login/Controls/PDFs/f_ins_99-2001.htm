Instructions for Governing/Policy Body Structure -- Form F

This section contains instructions for the following forms:

Form F-1 -- Governing Body Composition

Form F-2 -- Individual Board Member Information

Accessing the Forms: The LSC Grant Application Forms are accessible for all applicants who have submitted the Notice of Intent to Compete (Form H).  To access the forms, click Grants Applications from the main Legal Services Corporation Applicant Information Network (www.ain.lsc.gov) screen. This will take you to the Competitive Grants Applications menu. Then select Grant Application Forms and enter your LSC Applicant Id and the Password you used for submitting the Notice of Intent to Compete form.

After you have entered your Applicant ID and password, you will access the Grant Applications Forms Menu. Select Governing Body - Form F and click ok to continue. The Governing Body Forms menu contains the following:
Instructions and Definitions
Governing Body Composition - Form F-1
Individual Board Member Information - Form F-2

You can select either Form F-1 or Form F-2 and proceed.

Scrolling Forms: Some forms are larger than a single screen will contain and have a scroll bar along the right-hand margin of the screen to enable you to see the rest of the form. Clicking on the scroll bar will enable you to scroll down through a form. You may also use the Page-Down and Down-Arrow keys to scroll down through a form. Similarly, you may use the scroll bar or Page-Up and Up-Arrow keys to scroll back up through any form.

Entering Data: Each form has certain information pre-filled, including your organization's name and LSC Applicant ID. Each form will contain cells for entering the necessary data. To enter, click on the cell and type in the information. You may also use the Tab key to move to the next cell in a form. Pressing the Shift and Tab keys simultaneously will enable you to move to the previous cell.

Numeric Data: Some forms require entry of numeric information. These forms will not work properly with non-numeric data such as commas or spaces. Please do not enter commas or periods in numeric data, or leave a cell blank instead of entering zero. Entering commas, periods or any non-numeric data will cause an error message while saving. The system will not allow you to save such data. If one or more cells are blank, the Total row at the end of the form will display "NaN" which means that one or more cells contain a non-numeric value. You will not be able to submit a form with NaN and need to correct the problem before submitting.

Saving Data /Exiting Forms: Each form has buttons at the bottom for Saving and Exiting.

Printing Forms: You can use your browser to print the form that you are working on. Click on the print icon at the top of the screen when you are ready to print and click on OK to print.

Submitting Forms: Once you are certain that a form on which you have been working is complete, you may submit it to LSC by clicking on the SUBMIT button. Please do not press the SUBMIT button unless you are certain that you no longer wish to work on the form. After you have submitted a form to LSC, you will be unable to access it without calling LSC to ask that we un-submit the form.

All eligible persons, groups and entities must have a governing or policy body consistent with the requirements of 45 C.F.R. Part 1607 of the LSC Regulations. This regulation is designed to ensure that the governing or policy body of a recipient of LSC funds is well qualified to guide a recipient in efforts to provide high quality legal assistance and to ensure that the recipient is accountable to its clients.

The regulation defines governing and policy bodies separately. A governing body is a board of directors or other body with authority to govern the activities of a recipient receiving funds under Section 1006(a)(1)(A) of the LSC Act of 1974. A policy body is a policy board or other body established by a recipient to formulate and enforce policy with respect to the services provided under a grant or contract made under the Act. For a policy body to comply with the regulations, it must be approved by the Corporation through a waiver of the governing body regulations.

Applicants without a governing or policy body that complies with 45 C.F.R. Part 1607 must submit a plan for establishing a governing/policy body that complies with the LSC requirements. The applicant's governing/policy body plan should be submitted along with the proposal. For those applicants, submission of this plan shall be deemed submission of a waiver request under 45 C.F.R. Part 1607, and shall be subject to approval by LSC. All applicants must have a governing/policy body, that complies with 45 C.F.R. Part 1607, within 60 days of the date of the grant award. The general requirements for a governing /policy body follow.

  1. The majority of the members of the governing/policy body shall be attorney members appointed by the governing body(ies) of one or more State, county or municipal bar associations, the membership of which represents a majority of attorneys practicing law in the localities in which the applicant provides legal assistance.

  2. At least sixty percent (60 %) of the members of the body are attorneys.

  3. At least one-third (33.3%) of the members of the body were eligible to receive free legal services at the time of their appointment to the body.

  4. Appointments shall be made so as to insure that the governing/policy body reasonably reflects the interests of the eligible clients, the diversity of the legal community and the population of the areas served including race, ethnicity, gender and other similar factors.

 

Form F-1

All applicants must complete Form F-1.

I. Applicants that currently do not have a governing/policy body that conforms to the requirements of 45 C.F.R. Part 1607 should enter zero for each category on Form F-1 and must submit a plan for developing its governing/policy body. The applicant's governing/policy body plan should be submitted along with the proposal. Limit the governing/policy body plan to three double-space type written pages. Applicants are encouraged to thoroughly review 45 C.F.R. Part 1607 before preparing this plan. The governing/policy body plan should minimally address:

a. the total number of governing/policy body members that will be on the board;

b. the membership of the governing/policy body; (i.e., the number of attorneys appointed by bar associations, the number of other attorney governing/policy body members, the number of client governing/policy body members, and "other" governing/policy body members appointed to assure that the body as a whole reasonably reflects the diversity of the areas served);

c. how the governing/policy body will be established, (including identification of the bar association(s) that will appoint a majority of the governing/policy body members, and the steps that will be taken to assure ethnic and gender diversity) and;

d. the client organizations that the applicant anticipates will appoint income-eligible members for the governing/policy body.

II. If the applicant currently has a governing/policy body that conforms with the requirements of 45 C.F.R. Part 1607, applicant needs to enter the numbers of board members stipulated by Applicant's Bylaws or Articles of Incorporation.

Please enter the number of Bylaws Positions for each category (McCollum Attorneys, Other Attorneys, Client Members, Other members) on top of screen one.

Please also enter the number of current vacancies for each category based on the length of vacancy at the bottom.

Make sure to save the data before submitting.

Form F-2

Applicants who currently have a governing/policy body should also complete Form F-2 which details the individual board member information.

The first screen shows the list of board members you have entered on the Notice of Intent to Compete form. If there is no record this list will be empty. To add, delete or edit board member information, click on the 'Update' button at the end of the screen. The next screen will show you the list of all board members. Highlight the one you want to update and press the edit button. The individual member information screen allows you to edit Address, Gender, Phone/Fax numbers, Ethnicity, Appointing Organization and Type of member (i.e McCollum, Other Attorney, Client, etc). Use the 'Save and Exit' button to go back to the Board Members list.

To delete, highlight the member's name and press delete.

To add, press the add button. Enter the full name of the member (first name, middle initial, and last name) and click ok. Then enter the member's complete data on the next screen. Use the 'Save and Exit' button to save and go back to previous screen (select a member screen). Once you are done with updating and adding members press exit to go to the Form F-2 screen. This form now lists all your board members. Please print the form and check before submitting. Use the 'Submit' button at the bottom of the screen to submit this form.
