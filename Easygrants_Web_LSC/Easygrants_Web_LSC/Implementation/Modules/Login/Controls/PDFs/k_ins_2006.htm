Instructions for Technology Forms - Form K

This section contains instructions for the following technology forms:
Current Year Technology - Form K-1
Current Year Software Applications - Form K-2

Accessing the Forms: The LSC Grant Application Forms are accessible for all applicants who have submitted the Notice of Intent to Compete (Form H).  To access the forms, click Grant Application from the main Legal Services Corporation Applicant Information Network (www.ain.lsc.gov) screen. This will take you to the Competitive Grants Applications menu. Then select Grant Application Forms and enter your LSC Applicant ID and the Password you used for submitting the Notice of Intent to Compete form.

After you have entered your Applicant ID and password, you will access the Grant Applications Forms Menu. Select - Form K to continue.

The next screen displays the Technology Forms menu as follows:
Instructions and Definitions
Current Year Technology - Form K-1
Current Year Software Applications - Form K-2

To work on the forms, click in the circular button to the left of the item and press 'REPORT' to continue.  

Scrolling Forms: Because these forms are larger than a single screen will contain, each form has a scroll bar along the right-hand margin of the screen to enable you to see the rest of the form. Clicking on the scroll bar will enable you to scroll down through a form. You may also use the Page-Down and Down-Arrow keys to scroll down through a form. Similarly, you may use the scroll bar or Page-Up and Up-Arrow keys to scroll back up through any form.

Entering Data: Each form has certain information pre-filled, like applicant name and applicant ID. Each form will contain cells for entering information. To enter data, click on the cell which you want and type in the information. You can use the Tab key to move to the next cell in a form. Pressing the Shift and Tab keys simultaneously will enable you to move to the previous cell.

Numeric Data: Some forms require entry of numeric information. These forms will not work properly with non-numeric data such as commas or spaces. Please do not enter commas or periods in numeric data, or leave a cell blank instead of entering zero. Entering commas, periods or any non-numeric data will cause an error message while saving. The system will not allow you to save such data.

Saving Data: Each form has buttons at the bottom for Saving and Exiting. Please be sure to press the SAVE button prior to exiting the form on which you have been working. The EXIT button does not save information which you have entered. You may press the SAVE button at any time to save information which you have entered.

Exiting Forms: After saving the information which you have entered, you may click on the EXIT button to leave the form on which you have been working. You may exit from and return to any form as many times as you wish, provided that you have not clicked on the SUBMIT button.

Printing Forms: You can use your browser to print the form that you are working on. Click on the print icon at the top of the screen when you are ready to print.

Submitting Forms: Once you are certain that a form on which you have been working is complete, you may submit it to LSC by clicking on the SUBMIT button. As always, remember to print the forms before submitting. Please do not press the SUBMIT button unless you are certain that you no longer wish to work on the form. After you have submitted a form to LSC, you will be unable to access it without calling LSC to ask that we un-submit the form.

Forms K-1 and K-2 capture information about the applicant's staff and its computer and software capacity. All applicants must complete Forms K-1 and K-2. These forms are designed to collect information based on office technology in place at the time this application is completed. For LSC to obtain accurate information from these forms, all applicants must use consistent terms and concepts when compiling and submitting data. Please refer to the following definitions in responding to the technology questions in Forms K-1 and K-2.

Approved Computer: Defined as one that can perform all of the following functions: word processing, case management system access, access to time-keeping, Internet access, including the ability to download files from the Internet, and e-mail with the capacity to send and receive messages and files both internally and externally. 

 
Non-approved computer: Defined as one which cannot perform the above functions.

 
Automated attendant: A system feature that automatically answers incoming calls using a computer program instead of a live person. This feature may offer options to the caller, or may be connected to voice mail.

 
Automatic call distribution: A software application that delivers calls on a first come, first served basis. Calls are held and transferred automatically to the first available staff person assigned to those calls.

 
Bandwidth: The amount of data that can be transmitted in a fixed amount of time. For digital devices, the bandwidth is usually expressed in bits per second (bps) or bytes per second. For analog devices, the bandwidth is expressed in cycles per second, or Hertz (Hz).

 
Computer Network: A shared structure of computer hardware and software that serves users within a confined geographical area. It is made up of servers, workstations, a network operating system and a communications link. Servers are high-speed machines that hold programs and data shared by network users. The workstations are the users' personal computers, which perform stand-alone processing and access the network servers as required.

 
Interactive voice response: A system feature requiring a response from the caller, often using specifically designed recordings within the phone system.

 
E-mail: Short for electronic mail, this is a system that lets people send and receive messages and files with their computer both internally and externally. The system might be on a large network (such as the Internet), or over an office's own office network.

 
Phone system: The computer software and/or equipment used to handle telephone calls.

 
Routers: A device that forwards data along networks. A router is connected to at least two networks, commonly two local area networks or wide area networks. Routers are located at the places where networks connect. Routers use forwarding tables to determine the best path for forwarding data, and protocols to configure the best route between networks.

 
Self-direct: When a caller, whose call is answered by an automated attendant, can decide where their call will be answered or handled. Examples include choosing to go to a voice mailbox or selecting a staff person's extension.

 
Self-select: An ACD feature allowing callers to choose between options of pre-recorded messages.

 
Telephony integration: Telephony is the technology that electronically transmits voice, fax, email and other communications between two or more parties, integrating telephone and computer capabilities. Also referred to as Voice Over IP.

 
Voice Over IP: "Voice Over Internet Protocol" (VOIP).  This technology sends voice information in digital form instead of using traditional protocols of the public switched telephone.

 

I. Current Year Technology -- Form K-1

This form captures data on staffing and office technology.  Include employees who are paid in full or part with LSC funds.

Level of Computer -- report the number of case handlers in the first column and other staff in the second column.

Access to Email -- report the percent of case handlers in the first column and other staff in the second column.

Access to Automated Research - report the percent of case handlers who have access to automated research.

Degree of Connectivity -- respond to the yes/no questions regarding network capacity.

Internet Access -- respond to the yes/no questions regarding your offices internet connection.

Telephones  -- respond to the questions regarding your telephone system

II. Current Year Software Applications -- Form K-2

This form captures information about software applications currently in place. Please identify the types of applications along with the versions and the proportions of users in the following areas:

Operating Systems

E-Mail Software

Internet Browser

Word Processing Software

Spreadsheet Software

Database Software

Case Management

Timekeeping

Research and Accounting

Server / Network Software

Remote Access Software

Backup Procedures

Web Site Information

Technology Contact Information

Technology Plans

For Research & Accounting, Remote Access Software and Website Information identify the type of software and the version where applicable.

When entering the percent of staff using the software applications listed in the form, please use whole numbers without any punctuation such as decimal points. For example, enter the whole number 50 if the proportion of staff using an application is one-half.

Please make sure to save your work and print a copy before submitting the forms.
