LEGAL SERVICES CORPORATION ANNOUNCES FY 2000
GRANT AWARDS

WASHINGTON -- Legal Services Corporation (LSC) today announced the results of its FY 2000 grant competition, with service areas in thirty-four states, Micronesia, and the District of Columbia eligible for competitively awarded grants. The service areas that were eligible to apply for one- or two-year renewals did not compete in the competitive process and were all funded for their full renewal term.

As a result of the competitive process:

  • three-year grants will be awarded for most service areas in 17 states: Alaska, Connecticut, Delaware, Hawaii, Kansas, Idaho, Maine, Maryland, Micronesia, Minnesota, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Ohio, Rhode Island, Utah, Vermont, and Washington;
  • two-year grants will be awarded for most service areas in 14 states: Alabama, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Louisiana, Mississippi, Montana, New York, Nevada, Oklahoma, South Carolina, Tennessee, and Texas; and
  • one-year grants will be awarded to all service areas in Indiana, Nebraska, Pennsylvania and Virginia

The variations in grant terms have been made to encourage programs to continue developing methods to enhance effective statewide services for clients and to allow for consolidation of service areas consistent with state plans and prior LSC decisions. In some instances, shorter grant periods have been established in order to encourage improvement of program performance.

Grant award amounts will be announced as soon as the congressional appropriations process is completed for FY 2000.
