I.     IDENTIFYING AND ESTABLISHING PRIORITY LEGAL NEEDS



  1. Process Used to Identify the Most Critical Needs

    The process used by the applicant effectively identifies the most critical legal needs of eligible clients in the service area.

    Indicators:


    2. Sufficiently recent needs appraisal, e.g., 3 years.

    3. Annual or more frequent reviews to facilitate on-going consideration of and adjustments to new needs.

    4. Input is obtained through a combination of several methods to facilitate a thorough understanding of client needs- e.g., analysis of service area demographic data, personal interviews, group meetings, and written surveys.

    5. The numbers of people providing input, and the categories they represent, e.g., current clients, client eligible persons, program staff, governing body members, the private bar, judges, community groups, and public agencies, assure input from a broad and reasonably representative sample of those knowledgeable of client needs.

    6. Direct and indirect contacts with a range of community and client groups assures identification of a reasonably representative sample (in a practical, not scientific sense) of the service area client eligible population. Note that there may be fewer formal client organizations in rural areas.

I.     IDENTIFYING AND ESTABLISHING PRIORITY LEGAL NEEDS



  1. Conclusions on the Most Critical Needs

    The applicant's conclusions as to the most critical legal needs facing clients is based on the needs assessment data. To the extent applicable, these are differentiated by the major subgroups of the client population in the service area.

    Indicators:

    1. Clear and thorough description of the most critical legal needs include the most critical legal needs facing each major subgroup identified, or persuasive explanation and evidence of why the same needs are most critical for the entire client eligible population.

    2. Applicant explains how its critical needs conclusions followed from its needs assessment results.

    3. The determination of "most critical needs" is based on a rigorous, in-depth, and thorough analysis of pertinent information by staff and board, with meaningful participation by client representatives.

III.    MANAGEMENT AND LEGAL WORK RESOURCES



  1. Management Policies and Procedures

    1.    Budget Planning and Oversight

    Applicant's use of budget planning and oversight procedures assures the program's fiscal integrity while considering short and long term revenue needs and expenses.

    Indicators: Budget Planning

    1. Budget planning on a specific schedule.

    2. Budget planning that allocates resources based on programmatic and operational goals.

    3. Budget planning that considers capital as well as operating needs.

    4. Budget planning that considers likely revenue and expense changes in future years, i.e., multi-year budgeting.

    5. Budget planning is integrated with ongoing programmatic planning and review.

    6. Budget planning provides adequate reserves to assure continuity of operations.



    Indicators: Budget Oversight

    1. Governing body and executive director ensure that:

      1. Budget planning occurs for current and future years.

      2. Procedures exist for regular reporting and review that identifies budget variances, makes projections, and provides adjustments, as needed.

      3. Regular governing body finance or budget committee meetings occur.

      4. Procedures exist to analyze spending variances, to determine if priorities are being implemented, and to make adjustments, as needed.
