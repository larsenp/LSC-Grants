LSC Year 2001 Grants Competition

Instructions for Technology Forms - Form K

This section contains instructions for the following technology forms:
Current Year Technology - Form K-1
Current Year Software Applications - Form K-2

Accessing the Forms: The LSC Grant Application Forms are accessible for all applicants who have submitted the Notice of Intent to Compete (Form H).  To access the forms, click Grants Application from the main Legal Services Corporation Recipient Information Network (www.ain.lsc.gov) screen. This will take you to the Competitive Grants Applications menu. Then select Grant Application Forms and enter your LSC Applicant Id and the Password you used for submitting the Notice of Intent to Compete form.

After you have entered your Applicant ID and password, you will access the Grant Applications Forms Menu. Select - Form K to continue.

The next screen displays the Technology Forms menu as follows:
Instructions and Definitions
Current Year Technology - Form K-1
Current Year Software Applications - Form K-2

To select, click in the circular button to the left of the item and click 'OK' to continue.

Scrolling Forms: Because these forms are larger than a single screen will contain, each form has a scroll bar along the right-hand margin of the screen to enable you to see the rest of the form. Clicking on the scroll bar will enable you to scroll down through a form. You may also use the Page-Down and Down-Arrow keys to scroll down through a form. Similarly, you may use the scroll bar or Page-Up and Up-Arrow keys to scroll back up through any form.

Entering Data: Each form has certain information pre-filled, like recipient name and recipient ID. Each form will contain cells for entering information. To enter data, click on the cell which you want and type in the information. You can use the Tab key to move to the next cell in a form. Pressing the Shift and Tab keys simultaneously will enable you to move to the previous cell.

Numeric Data: Some forms require entry of numeric information. These forms will not work properly with non-numeric data such as commas or spaces. Please do not enter commas or periods in numeric data, or leave a cell blank instead of entering zero. Entering commas, periods or any non-numeric data will cause an error message while saving. The system will not allow you to save such data.

Saving Data: Each form has buttons at the bottom for Saving and Exiting. Please be sure to press the SAVE button prior to exiting the form on which you have been working. The EXIT button does not save information which you have entered. You may press the SAVE button at any time to save information which you have entered.

Exiting Forms: After saving the information which you have entered, you may click on the EXIT button to leave the form on which you have been working. You may exit from and return to any form as many times as you wish, provided that you have not clicked on the SUBMIT button.

Printing Forms: You can use your browser to print the form that you are working on. Click on the print icon at the top of the screen when you are ready to print and click on OK to print.

Submitting Forms: Once you are certain that a form on which you have been working is complete, you may submit it to LSC by clicking on the SUBMIT button. As always, remember to print the forms before submitting. Please do not press the SUBMIT button unless you are certain that you no longer wish to work on the form. After you have submitted a form to LSC, you will be unable to access it without calling LSC to ask that we un-submit the form.

Forms K-1 and K-2 capture information about the applicant's full-time permanent staff and its computer and software capacity. All applicants must complete Forms K-1 and K-2. These forms are designed to collect information based on office technology in place at the time this application is completed. For LSC to obtain accurate information from these forms, all applicants must use consistent terms and concepts when compiling and submitting data. Please refer to the following definitions in responding to the technology questions in Forms K-1 and K-2.

 

1) Level-I Computer Capacity includes:

a) 586 (or higher) multimedia system, b) 33.6 (or higher baud rate) modem, or network equivalent, c) color monitor, and d) Windows 3.1, or higher.

Level-II computer capacity includes:

a) 486 processor, b) 28.8 (or lower baud rate) modem, c) color monitor, and d) Windows 3.1 or DOS operating systems. (To meet Level-I or Level-II capacities, Macintosh computers must be fully interoperable with those system configurations)
2) E-mail

Short for electronic mail, this is a system that lets people send and receive messages with their computer. The system might be on a large network (such as the Internet), on a bulletin board, (such as CompuServe), or over an office's own office network.

3) Internet

The interconnection of computer networks in over 100 countries. The Internet is comprised of commercial, academic and government networks, and is open to the public for an access fee. It provides information on every subject known to humankind.

Internet Access

Access to the Internet, through an Internet service provider such as AOL, HandsNet, local Internet service providers, etc.
4) Computer Network

A shared structure of computer hardware and software that serves users within a confined geographical area. It is made up of servers, workstations, a network operating system and a communications link. Servers are high-speed machines that hold programs and data shared by network users. The workstations are the users' personal computers, which perform stand-alone processing and access the network servers as required.

 

I. Current Year Technology -- Form K-1

This form captures the number of full-time employees based on the office technology capacity. Please report only full-time permanent staff. Include full-time employees who are paid in full or part with LSC funds, full-time employees who are fully paid with non-LSC funds, and full-time volunteers.

Level of Computer -- report the number of full-time attorneys & paralegals in the first column and other full-time staff in the second column.

Access to E-mail -- report the number of staff who have access to email at different levels as categorized in the form.

Access to Internet -- report the number of staff who have access to the Internet.

Access to Automated Research - report the number of full-time attorneys & paralegals who have access to automated research.

Degree of Connectivity -- respond to the yes/no questions regarding network capacity.

II. Current Year Software Applications -- Form K-2

This form captures information about software applications currently in place. Please identify the types of applications along with the versions and the proportions of users in the following areas:

Operating Systems

E-Mail Software

Internet Browser

Word Processing Software

Spreadsheet Software

Database Software

For Case Management and Timekeeping identify the type of software and the version. For Research & Accounting identify the type of software.

When entering the proportions of staff using the software applications listed in the form, please use whole numbers without any punctuation such as decimal points. For example, enter the whole number 50 if the proportion of staff using an application is one-half.

Please make sure to save your work and print a copy before submitting the forms.
